Massive fire breaks out in residential building in Delhi's Tughlakabad; 3 dead, 2 injured

A devastating early-morning blaze engulfed a five-storey structure in the Tughlakabad Extension, trapping residents and triggering panic across the regularized colony.

HT
Hindustan Times Staff
Published on Jun 12, 2026 08:30 AM IST
Delhi Fire Service vehicles and rescue teams deployed at the five-storey apartment building in Tughlakabad Extension, southeast Delhi. (Staff Photo)

A massive fire broke out in a five-storey residential building in the Tughlakabad Extension area of southeast Delhi in the early hours of Friday, claiming the lives of three family members and leaving two others critically injured.

According to police and Delhi Fire Services (DFS) officials, the deceased have been identified as 28-year-old Pankaj Pandey, his 20-year-old sister Soni, and their 70-year-old grandmother, Sushila Devi. They were residents of the first floor. Two other family members, Pankaj’s mother Guddi Devi and younger sister Moni, sustained critical injuries and are currently undergoing intensive treatment at a local hospital.

Origin and Escalation

Preliminary investigations suggest that the blaze was triggered by an electrical short circuit on the ground floor parking lot. The fire erupted around 2:30 AM, quickly engulfing several parked vehicles, including an electric two-wheeler that was plugged into a charging outlet overnight.

Due to the flammable materials in the parking area, the flames spread with alarming speed, sending thick columns of toxic black smoke billowing up through the building's narrow stairwell. The staircase, which served as the only emergency exit, quickly became unusable, trapping residents on the upper floors.

"We woke up to screams and heavy smoke. There was no way to run down because the stairs were entirely filled with fire and thick smoke. It was terrifying." — A resident from the fourth floor

Sarees as Lifelines: The Local Rescue

Before fire tenders arrived, local residents took matters into their own hands. Exhibiting immense bravery, neighbors tied sarees together to form long ropes, throwing them up to windows and balconies to pull trapped residents to safety. Others formed human chains, throwing buckets of water to suppress the flames near lower windows.

A rescue team consisting of local police and DFS personnel eventually managed to retrieve eight residents from the upper floors using specialized ladders. Unfortunately, the three deceased victims had inhaled substantial amounts of toxic smoke and were declared dead upon arrival at the hospital.

Safety Violations and Expert Concerns

The tragic incident has once again turned the spotlight on severe building safety violations in regularized and unauthorized colonies across the national capital. Municipal experts noted that the five-storey structure violated standard height guidelines for such narrow lanes. Additionally, the complete absence of fire extinguishers, emergency exit paths, and proper ventilation turned the building into a fire trap.
